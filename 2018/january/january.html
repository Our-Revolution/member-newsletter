Our Revolution in the Press

The Unity Reform Commission met this past month to discuss the Democratic presidential nomination process. Although the odds were stacked against us, the commission made great strides in leveling the playing field. According to the Huffington Post, they “called for reducing the number of notoriously undemocratic superdelegates to the national convention from 712 to about 300, while the only democratic number would be zero.” Read more here. Now, as we enter 2018, it’s time to fight for the revolution. In Maryland, candidate Ben Jealous received endorsements from Communications Workers of America, Progressive Maryland, and more--making it seem all the more likely that we will have a Berniecrat in the Governor's seat. And in Texas, Our Revolution is endorsing and campaigning for candidates up and down the ballot causing real progressive change. In Philadelphia, new district attorney Larry Krasner is shaking up the establishment as he lives up to the true meaning of our revolution. The chronicle made a good point: “Some say Krasner's win may signal a broader shift to come in 2018.” Let’s make it happen!

Local Group Highlights

Western Maryland Our Revolution welcomed Our Revolution President Nina Turner to discuss how we can democratize the Democrats. Senator Turner spoke to over 100 attendees about the work Our Revolution is doing to reform the Democratic Party from within. She offered her insider’s account of her work on the DNC’s Unity Reform Commission and provided meaningful strategies for volunteers to get involved.

Senator Turner was also joined by Ben Jealous, our endorsed candidate for Governor of Maryland. Ben talked about how we can collectively create the circumstances that lead to a revolution. Bringing people together, identifying common goals, and building solidarity are just a few of the principles that he has been implementing in his campaign. He also emphasized how people power can always outweigh moneyed interests, focusing on the ways in which he has run his own campaign through grassroots organizing. Ben’s campaign brings the foundational principles of organizing and social movements together with the modern technology available today to build an effective campaign strategy that focuses on everyday and working people. Jealous is running a true people-powered campaign.

Medicare for All National Town Hall

Senator Sanders hosted three panels focusing on the health care crisis in the United States, the economic benefits of implementing Medicare for All, and an analysis from international health care experts answering questions directly from the audience. 1.1 million people watched as the panelists discussed the ways in which a Medicare for All system would actually cost the United States tremendously less than our current system. The United States presently spends $9,237 on health care per person while offering no guarantee of insurance. Norway, on the other hand, spends $6,537 per person, offers universal health care to all of their citizens, and allows for patients to choose whichever doctor or specialist works best for them.
