Races to Watch

On November 7, people will be going to the polls across the country to elect new local leaders.

In the 2017 General Election Our Revolution endorsed:

  • 59 candidates in eighteen states including,
    • 26 women candidates
    • 11 African and African-American candidates
    • 7 Latinx candidates
    • 4 Asian American/Pacific Islander candidates
    • 4 openly LGBTQ candidates
  • 1 ballot initiative, the Ohio Drug Price Relief Act

Some of the candidates Our Revolution and our local chapters have endorsed are:

  • Will Mbah - an environmental science graduate and students' rights advocate who became a U.S. citizen in 2015. He has pledged not to take any contributions from wealthy developers as he runs for Alderman in Somerville, Massachusetts.
  • E.J. Zita - a farmer, teacher and scientist who would user her position to ensure greater accountability and transparency, as well as to move toward renewable energy and resilience, as Port Commissioner in Olympia, Washington.
  • Erin Upchurch - a social worker, therapist, and community advocate who has dedicated her life to support and advocacy for underserved and marginalized communities, now running for the Columbus City Board of Education in Ohio.
  • Larry Krasner - a civil rights attorney who has long led the fight for criminal justice reform and ending the mass incarceration system, running for District Attorney in Philadelphia.

Inside Our Revolution

One of the most effective ways of convincing voters to turn out for progressive candidates and causes is to have a one-to-one conversation. At Our Revolution we have contact information for a vast network of progressives across the country - even in deeply conservative districts. Anyone with a phone and a computer can participate in voter outreach. Just a 2-3 minute call can inform a voter who already shares our values about a candidate or ballot measure in their area which they would be likely to support. Remember, Bernie won his first Mayoral election by just 10 votes. Every call you make really does matter! »
