A New Generation of Progressive Leaders

November has been a historic month for Our Revolution. Alongside local groups and volunteers across the country, we worked to elect a new generation of progressive leaders.

Twenty-seven Our Revolution endorsed candidates won their elections and brought the political revolution to almost every corner of the United States. In Virginia, Lee Carter defeated Virginia’s GOP Majority Whip by over 1,500 votes and Elizabeth Guzman became one of the first Latinas elected to Virginia’s House of Delegates. In Minnesota, Andrea Jenkins became the first out transgender woman elected to Minneapolis City Council. In Maine, for the first time ever, voters passed a ballot measure to expand Medicaid.

Every Vote Counts

Local groups, State Organizing Committees, and distributed volunteers worked in tandem to ensure every voter knew their polling place, voting hours, and who was on their ballot. Our Revolution’s amazing volunteers across the country put in countless hours during the November election cycle to make over 132,000 calls and send over 373,000 individual text messages. Thousands of voters got out and voted because of the hard work and dedication of Our Revolution’s volunteers - thank you for all that you do.

We have seen the direct results of the hard work of our volunteers and Our Revolution groups across the country, especially in the races won by tight margins. Here are just a few of the Our Revolution endorsed candidates who won by less than 1,000 votes:

In the Press

The Nation credited Somerville's local Our Revolution chapter with doing "the real work" of elections. The Huffington Post covered Lee Carter's victory in Virginia noting the "20,000 doors his campaign reached in the final four days before the election." The New Republic highlighted the "slew of local candidates backed by Our Revolution" who won "city council seats in Knoxville, Tennessee; Charlottesville, Virginia, and Somerville, Massachusetts."

Win or Lose

“We are building sustainable grassroots power in communities, win or lose.”
    — David Lynch, Our Revolution Volunteer

Not all victories are appropriately measured just by the results on election night. So many of our candidates created real grassroots movements in their communities by introducing new ideas and providing voters with an alternative to establishment politicians. These movements are pivotal to long-term change. Win or lose, the progress we have made this year is ground-breaking. Together we will continue to work to create a political revolution that will last and create needed change for all.
